# The Strange World of Deep Sea Creatures

A quick dive into the bizarre lifeforms that live in the deepest parts of our oceans.

## Scene 1: The Mystery Begins

Over 80% of our ocean remains unexplored. Beneath the surface, in crushing darkness, life has evolved in ways we are only beginning to understand.

**Visual**: Deep ocean darkness with bioluminescent creatures

## Scene 2: The Anglerfish

The anglerfish uses a glowing lure to attract prey in total darkness. Only females have this lure — and the males are tiny parasites that fuse to her body for life.

**Visual**: Anglerfish in dark water

## Scene 3: The Giant Isopod

These relatives of the common woodlouse can grow over 16 inches long in the deep sea, where scarcity of food makes them grow much larger than their shallow-water cousins.

**Visual**: Giant isopod on ocean floor

## Scene 4: The Vampire Squid

Despite its name, the vampire squid doesn't drink blood. It lives in oxygen-minimum zones and has eight arms connected by webbing. When threatened, it turns inside out.

**Visual**: Vampire squid defensive posture

## Scene 5: The Future of Discovery

New deep-sea exploration is revealing species every year. Scientists estimate millions of species remain undiscovered. The deep ocean might be Earth's last great frontier.
